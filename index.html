Project Outline: A job recommender system creates several user-profiles and uses the right recommendation technology based on the characteristics of target users to propose the best jobs or candidates to those users. Recommender systems are implemented in a variety of ways. A successful recommender system must be both accurate and quick in its predictions. As a result, the accuracy and performance of the various models must be assessed. The objective of this dissertation is to develop and parallelize three common job-search collaborative filtering algorithms. Three collaborative filtering algorithms are neighborhood-based, model-based methods, and Alternating Least Squares (ALS) algorithms. Parallelization is the most fundamental and effective method for reducing time. It takes to compute algorithms that may be done in parallel. These three algorithms will be leveraged by CareerBuilder.com which is one of the largest job boards in the world to generate high-quality suggestions for millions of users. The accuracy and performance of these approaches will be assessed as well.
